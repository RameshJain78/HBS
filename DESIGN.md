# Design System: HBS Consultancy

## Overview

**Creative North Star: "The Operations Confidence Deck"**

This visual system should feel like a decisive briefing prepared for business leaders who need to evaluate partners quickly. The tone is professional, direct, and grounded in delivery confidence rather than visual hype. Structure should favor scannability, fast trust formation, and clear next actions.

The atmosphere is set by the scene you selected: operations leaders reviewing HBS vendors at 10:00 AM in bright office light, focused on clarity and delivery confidence. This means a light-first surface, high legibility, confident typography contrast, and intentionally paced motion that signals speed without noise.

**Key Characteristics:**
- Clear information hierarchy with immediate value comprehension.
- Full-palette identity with disciplined usage, no neon accents.
- Professional motion language: subtle UI responsiveness plus one orchestrated hero entrance.
- Conversion-oriented narrative anchored on discovery-call booking.

## Colors

The palette should be full and deliberate, with 3-4 named color roles that communicate credibility, momentum, and operational precision.

### Named Rules
**The Strategic Full-Palette Rule.** Use a complete palette with named roles, each assigned to a distinct purpose (primary actions, support surfaces, trust cues, and emphasis).

**The No-Neon Rule.** Saturation can be strong, but fluorescent or neon-like color behavior is prohibited.

**The Trust-First Contrast Rule.** Every major text/surface pairing must pass practical readability checks for decision-makers scanning quickly.

Color values are intentionally unresolved in seed mode and will be finalized during implementation with extracted tokens.

## Elevation

This system is flat by default with restrained layering. Depth appears mainly through section contrast and subtle interaction states, while one choreographed hero reveal can establish brand momentum on first load.

### Named Rules
**The State-Driven Depth Rule.** Elevation is used as feedback, not decoration.

**The Hero-Pacing Rule.** Choreography is reserved for the opening sequence only; downstream interactions stay responsive and lightweight.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do:
- **Do** lead with credibility signals before decorative flourishes.
- **Do** make primary calls-to-action explicit, especially discovery-call booking.
- **Do** maintain clear semantic structure, visible focus states, and keyboard navigability.
- **Do** use purposeful, concise copy that communicates offerings (web design, ERP, WMS, traceability) within seconds.

### Don't:
- **Don't** use playful startup aesthetics.
- **Don't** use gimmicky animations.
- **Don't** use hype-heavy crypto-style visuals.
- **Don't** use visual noise or exaggerated claims.
- **Don't** use neon accents or fluorescent color behavior.
